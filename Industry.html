Exploring My Values at Intel

Industry Overview

The semiconductor industry powers the digital world—producing microchips that drive everything from smartphones to supercomputers. Intel is a global leader in this space, innovating in chip architecture, AI acceleration, and sustainable manufacturing.

My Role: Business Head

As a business head at Intel, I oversee strategic planning, product development, and global partnerships. My role bridges technical innovation with market execution, ensuring our solutions meet both performance and ethical standards.

Ethical Issue

One ethical challenge in this role is balancing profit with environmental responsibility. Semiconductor fabrication consumes significant energy and water—so decisions around sustainability, transparency, and long-term impact must be made with integrity.

Cybersecurity Issues

Two major cybersecurity concerns in this industry include:

  • Intellectual Property Theft: Advanced chip designs are prime targets for cyber espionage and corporate theft.
  • Supply Chain Vulnerabilities: Malicious actors may attempt to insert compromised components or firmware during manufacturing or distribution.

Cybersecurity Best Practices

To protect assets and data, Intel employs rigorous cybersecurity protocols:

  • Zero-trust architecture across internal networks
  • End-to-end encryption for sensitive communications
  • Regular audits and penetration testing
  • Employee training on phishing and social engineering threats
